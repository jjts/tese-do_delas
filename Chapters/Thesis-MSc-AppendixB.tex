\chapter{SEM Monitor Interface Reference}
\label{app_b:sem_monitor}

This appendix lists a small subset of SEM monitor commands and the typical responses observed during bring-up and automated fault-injection. The exact command set and report fields depend on the SEM version and device family; the examples below follow the UltraScale SEM controller monitor logs \cite{PG187}.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{|l|X|}
\hline
Command & Typical behaviour and response pattern \\
\hline
I & Enter Idle. Prints SC 00 and the prompt becomes I. Idle is the injection-accepting state. \\
\hline
O & Enter Observation. Prints SC 02 and the prompt becomes O. In mitigation modes, scanning and correction occur while observing. \\
\hline
S & Status summary. Prints a short report (including maximum frames and current state). \\
\hline
N packed\_addr & Inject one configuration bit flip at the addressed location. If injection is enabled, prints SC 10 then SC 00 and returns to I. \\
\hline
R fs & Software reset. Prints a banner, enters initialization (SC 01), reports initialization steps, then enters observation (SC 02). \\
\hline
T addr & Frame address translation. Prints a translated address representation on a single line. \\
\hline
Q addr & Query frame. Prints multiple lines of readback data (one per word), then returns to I. \\
\hline
D & Enter Detect Only. Prints SC 20 and the prompt becomes D. \\
\hline
U & Diagnostic scan. Prints SC 40, then reports detected errors (if any), then returns to I. \\
\hline
\end{tabularx}
\caption{Selected SEM monitor commands and expected response patterns \cite{PG187}.}
\label{tab:sem_cmds}
\end{table}

Example injection and state control (injection enabled):
\begin{lstlisting}
I N C000A098000
SC 10
SC 00
I O
SC 02
O
\end{lstlisting}

Example software reset and initialization sequence:
\begin{lstlisting}
I R 04
SEM_ULTRA_V3_1
SC 01
FS 04
AF 01
ICAP OK
RDBK OK
INIT OK
SC 02
O
\end{lstlisting}
